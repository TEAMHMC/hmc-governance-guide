Overview

Health Matters Clinic (HMC) is a community-centered health organization dedicated to advancing equitable access to care through integrated clinical services, prevention, and partnerships. The Board of Directors (“Board”) provides fiduciary oversight and strategic direction, while the Community Advisory Board (“CAB”) elevates lived experience and community priorities.

About HMC

  • Mission: Our mission is to break down barriers to health, ensuring that everyone, regardless of their background, has the opportunity to live their healthiest life.
  • Services: Primary and preventive care, behavioral health, care coordination, health education, and community outreach.
  • Communities Served: Underserved and underinsured individuals and families in our service area, with a focus on equity and access.
  • Approach: Evidence-informed practice, quality improvement, and cross-sector collaboration.

Roles & Expectations

All directors and advisors are expected to uphold fiduciary duties of care, loyalty, and obedience; maintain confidentiality; avoid and disclose conflicts of interest; and conduct themselves in a manner that advances the Organization’s mission and reputation.

Time & Participation

  • Quarterly full-board/CAB meetings; committees convene monthly or as needed.
  • Preparation: review agendas, financials, minutes, and materials in advance.
  • Attendance: target 75%+ of scheduled meetings; notify of anticipated absences.

Ambassadorial Duties

  • Champion the Organization’s mission and programs across networks.
  • Assist, as able, with fundraising, partnerships, and strategic visibility.

Community Advisory Board (CAB)

The CAB is an advisory body that provides lived-experience and sector expertise to inform programs, policy positions, community engagement, and quality improvement. CAB recommendations are non-binding but shall be recorded and considered by the Board and management.

Composition & Terms

  • Diverse representation across communities served; appointments made by the Board or its designee.
  • Standard term: two (2) years; renewable.

Scope

  • Program feedback and community needs assessment.
  • Outreach support, policy education, and stakeholder engagement.
  • Ad hoc working groups aligned to strategic initiatives.

Board of Directors (BOD)

The Board is the Organization’s governing body. It is responsible for strategic direction; oversight of financial and operational integrity; selection, support, and evaluation of the chief executive; and safeguarding of mission and assets.

Composition & Terms

  • Officers and directors as defined in the bylaws.
  • Standard term: three (3) years; typically renewable once consecutively.

Standing Committees

Executive; Governance & Nominations; Finance, Audit & Risk; Programs, Quality & Clinical Oversight; Workforce Development & Education; Development & Community Partnerships; Community Engagement & Advocacy. Charters may be adopted and amended by the Board.

Meetings & Schedule

  • Board: first Monday of January, April, July, and October, unless otherwise scheduled.
  • CAB: third Monday of March, June, September, and December.
  • Notice: Agendas and materials distributed at least seven (7) days in advance.

Standard Agenda

  1. Call to Order; Quorum
  2. Approval of Agenda & Prior Minutes
  3. Executive & Program Reports
  4. Financial Report & Audit/Compliance Updates
  5. Governance & Nominations
  6. Strategic Items / New Business
  7. Public Comment (as applicable)
  8. Adjournment

Conflict of Interest Policy

Adopted: October 18, 2025 • Next Review: October 2027

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Conflict of Interest Policy (the “Policy”) is to protect the interests of Health Matters Clinic (“HMC”) when it is contemplating a transaction or arrangement that might benefit the private interest of a director, officer, key employee, or advisory member (“Covered Person”).

2. Definitions

A Conflict of Interest exists when a Covered Person’s personal, professional, or financial interests could reasonably be perceived to interfere with the best interests of HMC. A Related Party Transaction is any transaction between HMC and a Covered Person or a Family Member or entity in which such person has a Material Financial Interest.

3. Duty to Disclose

Covered Persons shall disclose in writing all actual or potential conflicts to the Board (or Governance Committee) promptly and no later than annually via the Disclosure Form. Disclosures shall be updated as circumstances change.

4. Recusal & Determination

Any Covered Person with a potential conflict shall leave the meeting during discussion and voting on the matter. The disinterested directors shall determine whether a conflict exists and, if so, whether the proposed transaction is fair, reasonable, and in HMC’s best interests after considering alternatives.

5. Documentation

Minutes shall reflect disclosures, recusals, deliberations, alternatives considered, and the vote.

6. Violations

Failure to disclose may result in corrective action up to and including removal, consistent with bylaws and applicable law.

7. Annual Acknowledgment

All Covered Persons shall sign an annual acknowledgment affirming that they have received, read, and agree to comply with this Policy.

Code of Ethics

Adopted: October 18, 2025 • Next Review: October 2027

1. Values & Conduct

Directors, officers, staff, volunteers, and advisors shall conduct themselves with integrity, fairness, respect, and accountability, advancing HMC’s mission and avoiding any conduct that undermines public trust.

2. Confidentiality

Information obtained through service to HMC, including personal information of clients, donors, volunteers, and partners, shall be protected and used solely for legitimate organizational purposes.

3. Conflicts, Gifts & Gratuities

Conflicts must be disclosed and managed per the Conflict of Interest Policy. Gifts or favors that could reasonably influence decision-making may not be solicited or accepted.

4. Use of Assets & Records

HMC resources shall be used responsibly. Records shall be accurate, timely, and retained per retention schedules and applicable law.

5. Compliance & Reporting

Members shall comply with law and policy. Good-faith concerns about violations may be reported without fear of retaliation. Retaliation for good-faith reporting is prohibited.

6. Enforcement

Violations may result in corrective action up to and including removal, consistent with bylaws and due process.

Board Member Agreement

Effective for terms beginning on or after: October 18, 2025

1. Duties

As a director of HMC, I will discharge fiduciary duties of care, loyalty, and obedience; prepare for and attend meetings; serve on committees; and act in the best interests of HMC.

2. Participation

I will endeavor to attend at least 75% of board and committee meetings annually and be responsive to organizational communications.

3. Ethics & Conflicts

I will adhere to HMC’s Code of Ethics and Conflict of Interest Policy, complete annual disclosures, and recuse when appropriate.

4. Confidentiality & Communications

I will maintain confidentiality of nonpublic information and direct media inquiries to authorized spokespersons.

5. Support

I will act as an ambassador for HMC, assisting as able with strategic relationships and resource development. No mandatory give-get is imposed at this time.

6. Reimbursement

Reasonable, pre-approved expenses incurred on behalf of HMC will be reimbursed per policy.

7. Term & Separation

Terms are three (3) years unless otherwise stated. I may resign at any time by written notice; removal may occur per bylaws.

8. Acknowledgment

By signing, I affirm my commitment to this Agreement and HMC policies.

Frequently Asked Questions

\n
Who is eligible to serve?

Individuals who support HMC’s mission, can meet participation expectations, and uphold ethical standards. The Board considers professional expertise, community representation, and alignment with organizational values.

\n
What is the expected time commitment?

Board members average 3–5 hours per month; CAB members 2–3 hours. Committee work or special projects may add time seasonally.

\n
Are meetings hybrid or in-person?

Hybrid participation is supported. Meeting notices include connection details and materials at least seven (7) days in advance.

\n
Is there a financial contribution requirement?

No mandatory give-get is imposed at this time. Members act as ambassadors and connectors and may support development activities as able.

\n
How are conflicts of interest handled?

All potential conflicts must be disclosed. Affected members recuse from related deliberations and votes. Decisions are recorded in the minutes per policy.

\n
Are expenses reimbursed?

Reasonable, pre-approved expenses incurred on behalf of HMC are reimbursed consistent with policy.

\n
Whom do I contact with questions?

Email executive@healthmatters.clinic. You may also reach out via LinkedIn or YouTube.
